## Synthesized Guidelines for Development

Based on the full collection of pins, here are the key visual principles to follow when building the dopamine™ website:

### Photography Direction

- **Motion over stillness** — favor images that capture mid-movement, running, training in action
- **Natural light preferred** — sun-drenched, golden hour, or dramatic night lighting (streetlights, urban glow)
- **Close crops for intensity** — tight framing on faces, hands, muscles, shoes in motion
- **Community is central** — group shots matter as much as solo athletes; show togetherness
- **Urban/street context** — city streets, Venice Beach, NYC, Berlin — not sterile gym environments
- **Cinematic quality** — images should feel editorial, not stock. Think campaign photography, not catalog
- **Diverse representation** — mix of genders, body types, backgrounds; inclusive by default
- **Sweat and texture** — real effort visible; not over-retouched or overly polished

### Design Direction

- **Grid-based layouts** — social media grid thinking applied to web. Cohesive visual rhythm across pages
- **Editorial approach** — website should feel like a lookbook or magazine, not a standard fitness site
- **Brand benchmarks** — Nike Running campaigns, Equinox, Cadence Athletics, Bandit Running, Saucony/Ciele collaborations
- **Identity through consistency** — every visual touchpoint should reinforce the dopamine™ brand language
- **Content = brand** — imagery is not decoration; it carries the brand values of momentum, community, and real effort

### Reference Brands (from pins)
